Where it goes

Stake on the MorScan subnet earns the project a proportional share of the network's builder emissions pool (currently ~{{DAILY_EMISSIONS}} MOR/day network-wide, set by the protocol). Those emissions fund, concretely:

  • Real-time sync infrastructure and RPC capacity
  • Hosting and the global edge that serves the explorer and API
  • Monitoring and uptime
  • Continued development: the explorer, the API, and new analytics

That is what keeps the community's explorer free and running.

What you get

Staking is support first. What follows is the thank-you: capacity if you need it, recognition because the chain shows it, visibility for the biggest backers. The real return is the same for everyone - the tool stays free, live, and open.

How to stake

  1. Connect your wallet at the console. That is the whole registration - one signed message, nothing spent - and it issues your personal API key at the free tier.
  2. Stake MOR on the MorScan subnet from that same wallet, via the Morpheus dashboard. You can inspect the subnet first on the Builder explorer.
  3. Capacity follows automatically. Your key's limits re-check against your live on-chain stake every minute (and instantly on each connect). Unstake and they fall back; your principal stays yours throughout.
Stake on the MorScan subnet → No wallet or key yet? Connect at the console first.

Good to know

  • Unstaking from the MorScan subnet has a 90 day lock. That is this subnet's setting, chosen so the project can plan quarterly. It is not a protocol rule: the protocol minimum is 7 days and other subnets vary. Check any subnet's lock before staking.
  • Caps, precisely. Per-minute limits are exact. Daily and monthly volume caps apply (the daily cap is 5% of the monthly cap) and are enforced within a few minutes. Burst capacity tops out at 10,000/min.
  • Your MOR stays yours. Staking is support, not a payment or donation - your principal stays staked and withdrawable per the subnet terms.
